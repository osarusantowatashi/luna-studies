 Read the passage.


"What am I going to do?" Sabrina asked her father. "Look at this assignment we got at school today: 'Write about an important event that happened in your family.' What can I write about?" She sighed. "What if I can't find anything to write about?" 
 
"Why don't you go ask your grandmother to tell you about her wedding," her father suggested. 
Sabrina didn't think a wedding was an interesting event to write about, but she went to the next room and asked her grandmother to tell her about her wedding. 
 
"Oh, it was a special day," her grandmother said in a dreamy voice. "It was a very big wedding. We had invited four hundred guests, and everything was ready—the cake, the minister, the centerpieces, all so extravagant, but in good taste. Mine, of course. I was all dressed in a gorgeous white dress, and I was so happy and excited. My friends and I were laughing and joking and waiting for the groom to arrive… then his best friend came running to tell me he had been hurt in a car accident." 
 
Sabrina gasped. Her grandmother went on calmly, "Yes, you can imagine we were all rather anxious. I thought my make-up would be destroyed because I cried so much. Instead of going to the chapel, I went to the hospital to see him. He was unconscious for three days…" 
 
"That’s terrible!" Sabrina exclaimed. 
 
"Yes, it was," said her grandmother. "I barely left his side for three days until he finally woke up. He took one look at my face and started crying. He knew he had missed our wedding." 
 
"Poor Grandpa!" said Sabrina. 
 
"But I told him not to worry," her grandmother went on. "The doctors had told me he would make a full recovery but wouldn't be able to leave the bed for at least another week. So, I had the minister on hold. He came as soon as I let him know, and he married us right then and there, at the hospital. The nurses were our witnesses, and it was the best day of my life, just as I had dreamed it would be. That was all I needed." 
 
"I think I know what to write in my assignment," said Sabrina, hugging her grandmother. "Thank you."

What is the hidden message in the story?
A. It is more important that you marry the person you love than where or how you do it.
B. Getting married near a hospital bed is a wonderful idea.
C. Big weddings are a waste of time and money.
D. Even when you dream about something, sometimes it is necessary to give up on your dream.

Passage
In many cities around the world, community gardens have become increasingly popular. These shared spaces allow people to grow fruits, vegetables, and flowers together, even if they do not have their own yards. At first, community gardens were mainly created to help families save money on food. However, over time, people began to notice other important benefits.
One major advantage of community gardens is that they bring people together. Neighbors who may not have spoken before now work side by side, sharing tools, tips, and stories. This helps build stronger communities and friendships. In addition, children who participate in gardening often learn valuable life skills, such as responsibility and patience.
Community gardens also have environmental benefits. Plants help improve air quality by absorbing carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen. Gardens can also reduce the “heat island” effect in cities, where buildings and roads make urban areas much hotter than surrounding regions. Furthermore, growing food locally reduces the need for transportation, which lowers pollution.
Despite these benefits, community gardens are not always easy to maintain. They require regular care, cooperation among members, and access to water and sunlight. Still, many people believe the effort is worthwhile because of the positive impact these gardens have on both individuals and communities.

A. Community gardens are mainly created to help people save money on food.
B. Community gardens provide social, environmental, and personal benefits despite some challenges.
C. Gardening is a difficult activity that requires patience and responsibility.
D. Cities are becoming hotter due to buildings and roads.
