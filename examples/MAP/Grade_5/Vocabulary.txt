Example 3
Read the passage.
My parents recently moved to Chicago, and it was my first day in a new school. I had never been to a new school before; I had always gone to school with children I'd known my whole life. My heart was thumping furiously as I entered the unfamiliar building. One moment, I felt calm and optimistic, and the next, I felt like I was about to faint with nerves. All around me, children were laughing, slapping each other on the back, and greeting each other as old friends. I was the only one who did not spot a familiar face. I lowered my gaze and walked to the classroom. I tried to hope for the best, but it was difficult: it felt like my stomach was doing somersaults. My parents' voices echoed in my ears as I repeated their words softly to myself: "Just smile and be nice to everyone. Give them a chance to get to know you, and they will like you." Trying to believe that, I took a deep breath, clutched my hands tightly in my pockets, and through the roller coaster of emotions inside me, I entered the classroom.
It was still empty, as the bell hadn't rung yet. I took a seat and a moment later, a girl my age came in. "Hi," she said, smiling at me. "What's your name?" "Janice," I replied. "I'm Lelani," she said, sitting next to me. She asked where I was from and when I had moved to Chicago. We talked until the bell rang and the rest of the class entered. I couldn't wipe the smile off my face. I had made a friend!
What does the phrase "a roller coaster of emotions" mean?
A. the name of a ride in theme parks
B. extreme mood swings
C. challenging sports exercises
D. being very nervous
